Modelling covid-19 risk

There are many ways to model covid-19 transmission...

Transmission routes

COVID-19 can be transmitted between individuals through three routes: inhalation of aerosols, deposition of droplets on the facial mucous membranes and contact with infected surfaces transfered to the mucous membranes via hand or tool.

Exposure Model

The original model was developed in 'Relative contributions of transmission routes for COVID-19 among healthcare personnel providing patient care' (Jones, 2020). CEMRA uses a compartmental model of nine states within a room to estimate virus transport and fate for contact and inhalation transmission, implemented using a discrete-time Markov chain. Droplet transmission from coughing is based on respiratory droplet size distribution, extrapolated to the expiratory volume, with a probability of impacting the face in proportion to the surface area of the mucous membranes. The dose response-function is derived from an exponential function fitted to pooled data about SARS CoV infectivity in mice, The probability of infection is given as: P(infection) = 1 – exp(-d/410), where d is the dose. Limitations

Underestimating viral shedding Patients’ EB exhibited viral shedding of up to 1.59 × 10^6 RNA copies per hour (https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1201971221005671).
